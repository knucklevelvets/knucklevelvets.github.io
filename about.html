ABOUT

Hello! My name is Pen Fang, and I am a student at Cornell University studying art history and information science. I love art and media and creation, and I am trying to put more creation into my daily life. Some current areas of interest in my studies are: gender and sexuality studies, Greek tragedy, contemporary art, surrealism, film history, networks and algorithms.

GRIEF (A HOLE IN THE EARTH)

the keurig hums to life

drip drop, the sound of your heart

splattering against the floor

crimson, purple caressing your knees

now you’re alone running circles around

the hole in the earth

like clockwork, like crochet, stitch after stitch

your footsteps pitter-patter in loops

drip drop

flower field parting in cyclical motion

orbit of absence

there’s a hole in the earth

that you can’t avoid, won’t avoid, circle like water

down the drain

these bruises will fade

and when the pain stops cutting knife-hot

splintering your chest

then what will you have left?

ashes to ashes and dust to dust

you’re running in circles around a hole in the earth

i think i’ve forgotten how to be

without you by my side

so your chest kisses the dirt like a lover

lying spread eagle next to the hole in the earth

home in the earth

everything goes

your sun the moon sealed in a silent tomb

silent prayer called grief

the artist’s grave of permanence

i’ll never get you back i’ll never see you again

you don’t know what to do anymore

so you’re tracing circles around a hole in the earth

with your footprints over and over and over

your knees against the grass

purple. green. white knuckled fists

begging, begging

CURRENT PROJECTS

I am currently a college student and work on graphics for various clubs, including Cornell E.Motion and Cornell YDSA. I am working on making postcards and cards to send back to my hometown. My short-term goals including making more political art, and as always, trying to figure out what art means to me. My long-term plan is to paint another mural over the summer if I have the means, and synthesize my studies with creating art.

Here is a sketch/study I have done recently. I really wanted to capture the emotions. I wanted my art to be something more than just a pretty picture or a copy. Of course, I'm not sure where I am in terms of that, but I am going to keep experimenting until I find out. I'm trying to be more hybrid and physical with my creation, tying art with writing and more. My most recent sketchbook escapades include using dirt and water to paint a piece and a study in crayon.
